---
title: Margin and Leverage
description: How FluxPerp calculates collateral requirements and leverage limits.
---

FluxPerp uses USDC margin for all markets. A position's notional value is
divided by selected leverage to determine initial margin.

## Initial margin

```
initial_margin = position_notional / leverage
```

Example: a 1,000 USDC position at 10x leverage requires 100 USDC initial
margin.

## Cross and isolated margin

Cross margin shares collateral across positions. Isolated margin caps the
collateral allocated to one position. Cross margin improves capital efficiency;
isolated margin provides clearer maximum-loss boundaries.

## Leverage caps

| Market type | Max leverage |
|---|---|
| Major markets | 50x |
| Mid-cap markets | 20x |
| High-volatility markets | 10x |

<Callout type="warning">
Higher leverage reduces the distance between entry price and liquidation price.
</Callout>
